What Is an Employment Contract?

An employment contract is a legally binding agreement between an employer and an employee. It outlines the terms and conditions of employment, including:

  • Job title and description
  • Salary and payment terms
  • Working hours
  • Holiday entitlement
  • Notice periods
  • Confidentiality and restrictive covenants

UK Employment Law Requirements 2026

As of 2026, UK employers must provide a written statement of employment particulars from day one of employment. This is a legal requirement under the Employment Rights Act 1996 (as amended).

Key Changes in 2026

  • Day One Rights: All employees now receive full employment rights from their first day
  • Flexible Working: Right to request from day one (employer must respond within 2 months)
  • Paternity Leave: Shared Parental Leave reforms
  • Fire and Rehire: New statutory code of practice
  • Zero Hours Contracts: Greater transparency requirements

Free Employment Contract Template

EMPLOYMENT CONTRACT OF SERVICE

Between: [Employer Name] of [Address] ("the Employer")

And: [Employee Name] of [Address] ("the Employee")

1. COMMENCEMENT DATE

Your employment shall commence on [Date] ("the Start Date"). Your period of continuous employment begins on the Start Date.

2. JOB TITLE AND DUTIES

You will be employed as [Job Title]. You will report to [Manager Title] and perform such duties as are consistent with your position.

3. PLACE OF WORK

Your normal place of work will be [Address]. The Employer may require you to work at such other locations as the business may reasonably require.

4. REMUNERATION

Your salary will be £[Amount] per annum, payable monthly in arreys on or about the [Day] of each month.

5. HOURS OF WORK

Your normal working hours are [X] hours per week, [Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm]. You may be required to work additional hours as the needs of the business require.

6. HOLIDAY ENTITLEMENT

You are entitled to [X] days' paid holiday per annum, including the 8 public holidays in England and Wales.

7. SICKNESS AND SICK PAY

If you are absent from work due to sickness, you must notify [Manager] by [Time] on the first day of absence.

8. NOTICE PERIOD

During the first [X] months of employment, either party may terminate this contract with [X] weeks' notice.
